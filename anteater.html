Anteater Inside and Out

By Jackson Hicks

Introduction

Have you ever seen a furry, long snouted aardvark-like, but furry creature? If so, You have seen an anteater! Did you know that only 5,000 anteaters are left on earth? You will learn all about Anteaters and their diets, babies, habitat and more! If you want to know all about anteaters, keep reading this amazing and kid friendly book with tons of facts and information about these adorable animals!

Chapter 1: Food

Did you know that Anteaters eat mainly ants AND termites? An anteater can rip up ants and termite’s nests with their powerful forelimbs and claws, and then ingest them with their sticky tongue! They are surprisingly Omnivores, meaning they eat both plants and other animals. Anteaters are edentate animals, which means they have no teeth. But their long tongues are more than sufficient to lap up the 35,000 ants and termites they swallow whole each day! Research has found that giant anteaters can identify the particular species of ant or termite by smell. When feeding, sticky saliva coats the tongue. The 2-foot-long tongue is attached to the sternum and can flick in and out up to 150 times per minute. They rarely drink, but instead receive their water from the foods they eat or possibly moisture left on plants after rain! Anteaters are insectivores, which means they eat insects, hence the name. Anteaters can also eat other animals such as bees. Anteaters have tiny mouths with no teeth and do not chew their food. Instead, they have a very strong single- chambered stomach. In addition to ants and termites, anteaters also eat soft-bodied grubs, soft fruits, and birds' eggs. Anteaters in zoos also eat things like fruits, hard-boiled eggs, ground beef, and dog kibble! Anteaters aren't picky and are very awesome while eating.

Chapter 2: Habitat

You may not know what an anteater’s habitat is, so this subtopic is very handy. An anteater lives in South and Central America, so that is in south america and the area that connects north and south america. Central America borders the Pacific Ocean and the Caribbean Sea of the Atlantic Ocean. There are 7 countries in Central America, which are Belize, Costa Rica, El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, Nicaragua, and Panama. They are not found in Guatemala or El Savador, where they are considered to be extinct. They are also found in tropical savannas and forests from southern Mexico to Paraguay and northern Argentina. Central America are the countries south of Mexico and north of Colombia. South America is all the countries south of Panama. South America is Earth's fourth largest continent. It stretches from just above the equator down to the Antarctic. It is connected to North America by land. The Amazon River runs through South America and is surrounded by the world's largest rainforest! South America can be divided into three physical regions: mountains and highlands, river basins, and coastal plains. Mountains and coastal plains generally run in a north-south direction, while highlands and river basins generally run in an east-west direction. Anteaters are in a really cool spot, and are awesome there.

Chaper 3: Adorable babies

Anteater babies are in my opinion the cutest thing on earth! Read this subtopic to see what I'm talking about! Females normally give birth to one baby after a gestation period of 190 days. Anteater pups cling to their mother's back or legs while they are young, and sometimes continue to do so for up to a year. Anteaters are called Pups, and they are so tiny! An anteater can only give birth to one pup at a time. For the first 3 months the pup drinks milk from its mother. And after those three months the pup starts eating solids. The solids include, well, ANTS! Baby anteaters sleep as much as 15 hours each day. They are mostly found on their mom’s back when the adult is hunting for food. Anteaters have the absolute, most adorable babies which are so cute!

Conclusion

As you can see, anteaters are super interesting and awesome animals. You have learned all about their food, habitat, babies, and endangerment. If you want to learn more about them, go to your local library or a website! I hope you enjoyed
