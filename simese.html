Simese Cats

By Jackson Hicks

Introduction

Did you know that the siamese cats are one of the most royal types of cat breed? In this book you will learn about the siamese cat’s Appearance, History, and Pros and Cons! If you want to know all about these amazing animals, keep reading this amazing essay all about SIAMESE CATS!

Chapter 1: Appearance

The siamese cats are medium cats, and weigh from 8-15 lbs. (pounds). There are 2 types of Siamese: Traditional and Extreme. Both of them share the same colorations, it’s just their body is different. Cat associations prefer the extreme type, rather than the traditional. So much so that the traditional types are not to be competed as Siamese cats, but they are called Thai Cats. Their eye color is blue and almond-shaped, and their body colors are tan and brown, with partial black on the tail, paws and head. This type of coloration is called ‘point’. The CFA only accepts the following colors though: seal, chocolate, lavender and blue. The tail is long and tapers to a point. The Siamese coat is short, glossy and lies close to the body. The beauty of the Siamese cat is the look of the slender body, the blue eyes and the contrast between the color on the body and the darker color of the extremities, which can be really loved for.

Chapter 2: History

Siamese cats originated in Thailand, an area once known as Siam. Some stories suggest that they were first raised by Buddhist monks. Other tales say that the first Siamese cats were adopted by royalty. The first Siamese cats in Europe were a gift from the King of Siam to the English consulate general in Bangkok in the late 1800s. They were popular pets for royalty, AND for religious leaders. They were familiar sights in Buddhist temples for an intriguing reason. Siamese cats were considered sacred, because everyone thought they carried the souls of passed away loved ones. As a result, they were praised like kings and queens. Siamese cats were praised and adored in their homeland. For this reason they were rarely given to foreigners. But somehow by the 1800s they managed to make their way to england. The siamese was greatly appreciated for its looks! In 1871 they participated in the first modern feline exhibition! This show took place right in London’s Crystal palace! Surprisingly a journalist wrote afterward that Siamese cats are unnatural and a nightmare of a cat. As did the British because the Siamese cat quickly became very popular. They were especially sought after because most of them had crossed eyes and twisted tails. Sadly, they are considered faults nowadays. Siamese cats quickly conquered england. Thus, it was only a matter of time until they arrived in america.
