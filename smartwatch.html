Smart Watches

By Quentin Kirschner

Introduction

Ring! Ring! Your phone is ringing and it’s on the counter, but you just sat down. Your about to get up but then remember you just received your new smartwatch. You pick your wrist up and press a button the smart watches touch screen and receive and complete the phone call from your couch. You’ve probably heard about these wonderful compact computer on your wrist, but they are worth learning more about their history, types, how they work, and their future.

Chapter 1: History

The smartwatch that connects to your phone and allows you to do so many things wasn’t always this way. Infact, the first smartwatch was only a calculator. Introduced in 1976 and known as the Pulsar Time Computer Calculator. It was merely a diamond on your wrist retailing for $2,100. The luxury design was covered in 18-karat gold and retailed for a mind boggling $4,000. Today that’s equal to 25,000 dollars. The first smartwatch that could connect to your phone was the Microsoft SPOT (Small Personal Object Technology). Which provided live weather, stock prices, sport results, news and much more. The user could also receive email, but could not respond. As you can see the smartwatch has an interesting history.

Chapter 2: Diffrent kinds

There are many different types of smart watches including phone watches, general watches, and outdoors watches. The most popular smartwatch is by far just a general smartwatch. There are many reasons they are most popular, but one of the most important ones are they have a lower price range. They can also complete almost everything you need. The second most popular is the phone watch. One reason it comes in second is because people look at the price and normally automatically walk away. They are actually pretty amazing because they are just a phone that fits onto your wrist, but are less expensive than an actual phone. They have almost all the same functions. Not far behind is the outdoors watch. This type of watch normally doesn't have all the same functions as a general or phone watch. Its main purpose though, is to count your steps, keep track of your vitals and provide the weather forecast, and time. One more function it has is an extremely extended battery life incase you don’t have access to a charger for a while. As you can see there are three main types of watches that all serve different purposes and complete different things.

Chaper 3: How they work

You might think General Smart watches are complex and require lots of parts and pieces, but they aren’t as complicated as you might think. The first thing is how a smart watch see’s your vitals. First off, have you noticed that small LED light on your watch that’s usually red or green and sensors around it. These measure the changes of blood flow and how many times a minute your heart beats. It can also do the same thing with your blood oxygen saturation levels. If you're wondering how you receive text messages and phone calls then keep reading. The smart watch connects to your cell phone with bluetooth. That way it can receive live news, weather and much more through your phone's connection to the internet. So if your phone gets called, it can send it through bluetooth to your watch. As you can see smart watches work in interesting ways.

Chapter 4: Future

Smart watches have a bright future with us. Possibly in the future cell phones will be obsolete and we will just use are watch. Infact, they already have the technology to shrink a phone down to the size of your wrist. So, it’s just a matter of time. Also in a few years maybe we can have projectors on are watches. To watch videos and to do video calls. Someday you might have a watch that can do everything. All I know though is that we have a bright future with technology.

Conclusion

As you can see smart watches make all of are life's easier. Now that you have learned more about smart watche's history, different kinds, How they work, and their future. Now that you have learned some general information about smartwatches go to your local library and find out more about them. I hope you enjoyed
